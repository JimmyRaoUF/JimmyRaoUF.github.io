Capturing Daily Life, Respectfully

This interactive application breaks down a week-long study designed to understand personal interests through passive audio capture. Explore the three phases of our participant-centric protocol, built from the ground up on a foundation of trust, agency, and an uncompromising commitment to privacy.

PHASE 1

Onboarding & The Privacy Contract

The study begins with an in-person session designed to establish trust and transparency. Participants are not just subjects; they are collaborators who are given full control from the very start. This phase sets the stage for the entire week.

Welcome & Introduction

Participants are welcomed and given a clear overview of the study's goals. The voluntary nature is emphasized, ensuring they feel comfortable and informed.

PHASE 2

Data Collection in the Wild

For one week, participants wear the smartwatch as they go about their daily lives. The system is designed to be unobtrusive, passively capturing potential "interest moments" based on intelligent sensor triggers, not constant surveillance.

Passive & Unobtrusive

The system works in the background. Subtle haptic cues (vibrations) provide awareness of recordings without being socially intrusive. An optional, temporary prompt on the watch allows for immediate discarding of a sensitive snippet, giving participants real-time agency.

Intelligent Sensor Triggers

Recording is not random. It's activated by specific situations that are likely to contain engaged, interest-relevant conversation.

1. Context Changes

Sensors detect shifts in location or activity (e.g., leaving a dorm, sitting down in a cafe) which often precede new social interactions.

2. Gesticulation & Arousal

Detects animated hand movements and elevated heart rate‚Äîstrong indicators of engaged, emotionally salient conversation.

3. Focused Individual Activity

Captures moments of "thinking aloud" during focused tasks (e.g., studying, hobbies), offering a window into cognitive engagement.

Context: Student Time Use

The study targets college students because their daily lives are rich with the social interactions where interests are expressed. This chart, based on American Time Use Survey data, illustrates the significant time young adults dedicate to social activities.

PHASE 3

The "Locked-Box" Curation Session

The final session is where the privacy promises are fulfilled. Participants enter a secure, offline environment to review their data and become the sole curators of what is shared with the research team.

CONCLUSION

Core Principles of the Protocol

This entire methodology is built on three pillars that work together to create an ethical, effective, and user-centric research process.

Absolute Privacy Control

The protocol inverts the typical data collection model. By giving participants ultimate control and making deletion the default, we build a foundation of trust essential for collecting authentic data.

Minimum Effort & Burden

From passive collection to AI-assisted text-first review, the process is designed to be as seamless and low-effort as possible, respecting the participant's time and cognitive energy.
